About This Game

Instructions

Hobnob is a game about making friends and remembering them.

Players take turns introducing characters by coming up with names and fun facts about each one.

When it's your turn, show everyone the face, then click "record", say the name or fun fact, then click stop. You can check and re-record it, or just click "next".

At harder difficulty levels, you also introduce a fun fact.

After several characters have been introduced, you'll instead be shown a character you met earlier. You can try guess the name and all the other facts that have been introduced for that character. Then, either click "Wrong" to end your turn. or click "Right" to give yourself a point and then introduce some new information: either a new fact about this character or a new character (this is random, based on the "depth" option).

If players disagree on the correct answer, click "check" to replay the name and all facts.

If you don't remember the name and all the facts, instead of guessing you can "defer" to another player. If they get it right, they get a point and introduce a new fact or character. But if they get it wrong, you get a point.

Turns and scores are tracked with the tabs at the top of the screen. As soon as a player fills up their score meter, they win.

Note: Part of the fun is getting creative and witty when introducing characters. But if a name or fact is too obscure or offensive, other players can veto it. If half of the players vote it down, the player must record something different. Likewise, if a player is close enough when guessing a weird name or fact, the other players can vote to accept it anyway.
